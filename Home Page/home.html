SDG 11, or Sustainable Development Goal 11, focuses on making cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable. It aims to address the challenges of rapid urbanization and promote urban development that benefits all residents. This includes ensuring access to basic services, affordable housing, sustainable transport, and public spaces, while also reducing environmental impact and preserving cultural and natural heritage.


Energy Use- Energy Use is the amount and type of energy consumed in homes, transport, industries, and public services. Sustainable energy use focuses on efficiency, reducing waste, and shifting to renewable sources like solar and wind. This helps cut emissions, lower costs, and improve a city’s environmental and economic resilience.


Waste Management- Waste Management involves collecting, processing, recycling, and safely disposing of waste from homes, businesses, and industries. Sustainable waste practices reduce pollution, conserve resources, and protect health. Strategies like recycling, composting, and waste-to-energy conversion help cities minimize landfill use, lower emissions, and create cleaner, healthier urban environments.


Air Quality- Air Quality describes how clean or polluted the air is, based on levels of harmful substances like particulate matter, nitrogen oxides, and ozone. In cities, it is influenced by traffic, industries, and waste burning. Good air quality supports health, reduces climate impacts, and improves overall urban livability.
